3x Examples of Noteworthy Interactivity

1. The Crossroad

I think that the crossroad, the kind where it's a full urban junction during peak hour, is one of the densest examples of overlapping interactivity I can think of that has nothing to do with a screen.

What I found noteworthy was how many interfaces are active at once, nested inside each other. For one, the driver is inside a car, which is itself a mechanical system they are operating through pedals, a wheel, a gearstick, and many other buttons.

But simultaneously, that same driver is in dialogue with the traffic light, and they are reading and being read by every other driver, cyclist, pedestrian in the space. It's an extraordinarily complex point of interactivity and almost entirely unspoken, only conveyed through micro-interactions like signals.

2. Cooking

In the kitchen I realised I've been interacting with a myriad of interfaces. What interests me is how much of this is unconscious until I really thought hard about points of interactivity. Yet operating all of these systems required learning at some point - There was a version of younger me that didn't know intuitively that a knob rotates, that a handle pulls, that a button depresses and clicks.

I think about how these affordances shape mental models overtime - the instincts I built through failure and repetition until the interface stopped being visible and became just the thing I do to make something happen.

If I were to be presented with a button, I would have muscle memory and assumptions of how to interact with it, compared to something like a rock.

3. Snorkeling

This one is slightly weird, I thought about water as an interface, one with unclear but infinite affordances (since it's a liquid?). But when I'm swimming, does water become the interface and the medium at the same time, since I'm moving through it? or does my body become the interface? It's all a bit blurred.

Also I like the idea that underwater, the usual feedback loops between intention and outcome are all slightly delayed or slightly displaced. It's like you need to recalibrate your body like a device.
