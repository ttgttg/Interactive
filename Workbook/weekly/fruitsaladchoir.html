Capacitive Touch Sensing

For our last physical computing class, we did capacitive touch sensing with potato, foil, and leaves. It was really fun to adjust thresholds and sound levels. Tuning threshold values higher made the objects more sensitive to electrical signals, and we could change the note pitch for each object too.

Other ideas / applications I had in mind:

- Conductive ink printed onto clothing or paper (like the interactive fabric shown in class)
- A plant that plays music when you water it, using moisture as the signal

Looking back at physical computing as a whole, it's been a really eye-opening introduction to working with hardware. Coming in with mostly a software background, the idea of breadboards and resistors felt pretty foreign, but by the end it felt a lot more approachable. What intrigued me was the possibilities of affordance in these explorations. A potato, a foil ball, a leaf isn't a button, until it is. It was great to jump out of ordinary interactive surfaces, and made me feel like I'd now be better at solving escape room puzzles.
